Regular competitive analysis ensures you stay ahead of market shifts and maintain differentiation.

## 9. Competitive Response Playbook

### 9.1 Monitoring Triggers

**Trigger Events** (requiring immediate analysis/response):
- Major product launch by competitor
- Significant funding announcement
- M&A activity (acquisition or merger)
- Executive leadership change
- Major customer win/loss (publicly announced)
- Pricing change
- Market entry into new segment/geography

**Escalation Path**:
1. Competitive Intelligence Lead identifies trigger
2. Alert stakeholders (Product, Marketing, Sales)
3. Rapid assessment (within 24-48 hours)
4. Response plan (if needed) within 1 week

### 9.2 Response Strategies

**If competitor launches feature we lack**:
- [ ] Accelerate roadmap to match
- [ ] Emphasize our superior implementation of adjacent feature
- [ ] Position as "nice to have" vs. our core strengths

## Agent Notes

### For Competitive Analyst
- Update competitor profiles quarterly (minimally) or when significant changes occur
- Use mix of public intelligence and customer feedback (win/loss analysis)
- Avoid feature parity trap - focus on differentiation, not matching everything
- Track competitor job postings to infer product roadmap and strategic priorities

### For Market Researcher
- Combine competitive analysis with market trend analysis (technology shifts, buyer behavior)
- Validate competitor claims through customer reviews and independent testing
- Identify white space opportunities (underserved segments or use cases)

### For Product Marketer
- Translate competitive insights into actionable battle cards and sales enablement
- Use competitive analysis to sharpen positioning and messaging
- Ensure competitive intelligence is accessible to sales team (wiki, CRM, Slack)
- Create "trap setting" strategies (emphasize differentiators that competitors can't easily match)
